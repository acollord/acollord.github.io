In a Nutshell:

My research is focused on reducing the cost of solar energy. To do this, I use two different approaches: (1) studying new materials, like copper zinc tin sulfoselenide (CZTSSe), that are made of abundant, low-cost elements; and (2) developing printable inks as a new way to deposit these materials. CZTSSe is a relatively new material, and we need to better understand it to improve the efficiency. To speed up this process, I developed a combinatorial screening process that allows us to evaluate thousands of samples in the time it formerly took to test one. Using this technique I have mapped out how the properties of CZTSSe change with the relative quantites of copper, zinc, and tin. I've also looked at how a broad array of different extrinsic elements (such as iron, antimony, sodium, etc) effect CZTSSe.
