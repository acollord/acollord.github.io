My Research: 

I am currently a PhD student in chemical engineering at the University of Washington

My research is focused on reducing the cost of solar energy. To do this, I use two different approaches: (1) studying new materials, like copper zinc tin sulfoselenide (CZTSSe), that are made of abundant, low-cost elements; and (2) developing printable inks as a new way to deposit these materials. CZTSSe is a relatively new material and we don’t yet know how to make very efficient solar cells from it. To speed up this process, I developed a unique combinatorial screening process that allows us to evaluate thousands of samples in the time it formerly took to test one. Using this process, I have mapped out how the optoelectronic properties of CZTSSe change when we vary the relative quantities of the native elements (copper, zinc, and tin), or add extrinsic elements (like iron, sodium, antimony, and many many others).
